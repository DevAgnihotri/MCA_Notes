#### Limitations of SimpleDB

- Limited query expressiveness and fewer filter/aggregation capabilities than modern datastores.
- No full multi-item ACID transactions; constrained consistency model (eventual consistency for many operations).
- Restricted indexing options and less control over query performance tuning.
- Not optimized for very large datasets or very high throughput; scaling is more limited compared to newer services (e.g., DynamoDB, Cloud Datastore).
- Item and domain size/limits exist (practical limits on attributes and item size).
- Fewer advanced features (backup/restore, fine-grained security controls, analytics integrations) compared to contemporary managed datastores.
- AWS recommends newer services for most production workloads; SimpleDB is suitable mainly for simple, lightweight use cases.
